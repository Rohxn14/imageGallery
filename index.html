Iconic Places in the World

Colosseum

The Colosseum in Rome, Italy is one of the world’s most iconic places and is widely recognized as a symbol of the ancient Roman Empire. The Colosseum was the center of entertainment in ancient Rome, with events lasting for up to 100 days. It is estimated that the Colosseum was used for 390 years to host these gladiatorial fights. Despite its age, the Colosseum remains an impressive structure, with 80 entrances that lead to different sections of the amphitheater.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an iconic monument located on the banks of the Yamuna River in Agra, northern India. The name, Taj Mahal, means “crown of palaces” in Persian and is a testament to the timeless love between Emperor Shah Jahan and his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. In 1632, Shah Jahan ordered the construction of a tomb to be built in memory of his wife, and the Taj Mahal was born.

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is one of the most iconic places in the world and a must-visit for anyone traveling to Paris. Designed by Auguste Eiffel and a team of engineers, the tower stands at a height of 324 meters (1062 feet). Visitors to the Eiffel Tower have the option to take the stairs up to the second-floor viewing platform, with a total of 704 steps to climb.

The Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is a towering symbol of freedom and democracy, rising 305 feet tall in the waters of New York Harbor. It was designed by French sculptor Frederic Auguste Bartholdi and the iron structure was engineered by Alexandre Gustave Eiffel, who was also responsible for the iconic Eiffel Tower.

The Great Wall of China

As one of the seven wonders of the world, the Great Wall of China is an architectural marvel that has stood the test of time. Stretching across the country in sections over an impressive 21,196 km/13,171 miles, this iconic landmark is renowned for its size and historical significance. Construction of the wall began more than 2,000 years ago and was completed in 1644. It was built using a combination of materials, including stones, bricks, tiles, earth, and even wood. The wall’s impressive length is further enhanced by the presence of over 20,000 watchtowers, which were used to keep watch over the surrounding lands.

Great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza is one of the most iconic places in Egypt and is a testament to the ingenuity of ancient engineers. It is the only one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World that still exists today and is considered one of the greatest architectural achievements of all time. Located near Cairo, the capital of Egypt, the Great Pyramid of Giza is a massive structure made of stone and bricks that stands as a testament to the skill and determination of its builders. Despite the lack of modern equipment, the pyramids were built using only manual labor, a testament to the strength and resilience of the ancient Egyptian people.

Machu Pichu

Machu Picchu, also known as “The Lost City of the Incas,” is one of the most iconic places in Peru and a must-see for any traveler. The purpose of Machu Picchu is a subject of debate, with some believing it was a sacred site while others believe it served as a summer retreat for an Inca emperor. What is known, however, is that it was built during the 14th century and was once home to over 1,000 people.

Stonehenge

Stonehenge is one of the world’s most iconic places and a popular tourist destination located in Salisbury, England. This prehistoric monument consists of multiple stones and is believed to have been built between 3000 BC to 2000 BC. Despite many years of study and research, the actual purpose and method used to bring the huge stones to the site remain a mystery to this day.

Sydney Opera

The Sydney Opera House is one of the most iconic places in Australia and is widely recognized as a symbol of the country. Located at Bennelong Point on Sydney Harbour, it is a bustling arts center that hosts more than 1,500 performances each year. The opera house features a concert hall with 2,700 seats, a drama theater, a playhouse, a forecourt, and a studio, making it a hub of cultural activity.

Tower of Pisa

The Leaning Tower of Pisa is a symbol of architectural prowess and a testament to the ingenuity of medieval builders. It is a world-renowned landmark that draws millions of tourists every year to the Italian city of Pisa. At the time of its completion, the tower stood 60 meters tall, or 196 feet. However, the tower’s lean, caused by the soft and unstable soil beneath it, has reduced its height on the lowest side to less than 56 meters, or 184 feet.
